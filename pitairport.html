Pittsburgh International Airport

An effort to improve the customer experience at PIT.

Context

The Pittsburgh International Airport is undertaking a massive transformation and is coming up with a new terminal set to go live in 2023. The airport anticipates the number of travellers to increase significantly in the coming years and is therefore looking into how to reduce wait times in queues such as the TSA, boarding, etc.

Problem

Long wait times in queues are frustrating and lead to an overall bad customer experience through the airport. How might we reduce a users’ frustration and anxiety in the security line by providing them more transparency, control, & efficiency?

Solution

An application to provide users transparency into the airport processes & security line wait times so that they can better plan their airport journey. It supports two different channels 1. A chatbot and 2. A mobile application.

Why a chatbot?
A chat bot has a lower barrier to entry than a full fledged app in the sense:

  • It doesn't need to be downloaded like an app onto your phone priorhand.
  • It doesn't need a smartphone and could work on just SMS.
  • The communication is natural language based which doesn't require prior learning.
Why Facebook messenger & SMS?
An airport has a very diverse range of users with different phones, needs, tech-savyness etc. We needed a platform that could satisfy the requirements of all which is why we built this on Facebook Messenger since it is independent of the underlying Operating System (iOS or Android). This be can scaled down to SMS or leverage the capabilities of rich messaging systems to form an MMS bot.
