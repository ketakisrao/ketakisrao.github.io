Say Cheese!

Concept

Cheeses have been the love of my life since I've started tasting food! Also, I wanted to experiment with SAP's new visualisation tool called Lumira. So I made this storyboard full of cheeses. (Well according to Google, there are more than 4,500 varieties of cheeses, wasn't possible to get 'em all!)
Here is the dataset I made by extracting information on each of the popular cheeses from Google using Scrapy (Python).

Visualisations

Type: Word Cloud

What it represents? It shows the popularity of each cheese.

Why I chose this? A word cloud was the best representation to show the name of the cheese based on how popular it is. The more popular a cheese, the more it's name stands out. In this case, Mozzarella is the most popularly used cheese and hence is the biggest amongst all.

Type: Area Chart

What it represents? Some cheeses are left for a certain duration under microbial action to let the cheese and age. The chart shows the aging time for each cheese.

Why I chose this? The fresh cheeses do not need anytime to age and therefore the valleys in an area chart would show the fresh cheeses and the spikes would show the ones that need time to age. Using the varied colours made it look truly scintillating!

Type: Bubble Chart

What it represents? Showcases popular vs cost of each cheese.

Why I chose this? I needed a chart that could represent both these dimensions and a bubble chart was best suited. The more above a cheese from the baseline, the more popular it is. To make it more interesting, I've represented the cost by the size of the bubble. It is much easier to figure out how costly a cheese is by the size of it's corresponding bubble.

Type: Radar Chart

What it represents? Shows the cholesterol level of each cheese.

Why I chose this? Although a radar chart represents multivariate data, I made this one show the different cholesterol levels in each cheese to make the story more interesting.

Type: 3D Bar Chart

What it represents? The mineral content of different minerals in each cheese.

Why I chose this? It was the perfect chart to represent the mineral vs cheese matrix.

Type: Geo-pie Chart

What it represents? The chart shows the biggest producers of cheese in the world.

Why I chose this? A geo-pie chart was perfect to represent all the metrics. The size of the pie chart shows the size of production of cheese in each country. Ideally, the pie chart should have shown the popular cheeses in each country but I couldn't get it to represent those.

Type: Grouped Bar Chart

What it represents? Fat to protein comparison of each cheese.

Why I chose this? A grouped bar chart would allow easy comparison between the fat and protein content of each cheese.

This is the final story, and it's all about Cheese!
